Bill Kenwright Career:

In his career in the theater industry:

Kenwright established himself as one of the UK’s most successful theatre producers, gaining recognition for the enduring West End hit “Blood Brothers” and the record-breaking UK tour of “Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat.”

In the music realm:

Kenwright operated his own record label, Bill Kenwright Records, which released three albums by February 2008.

Notable recordings included the London Palladium cast recording of “Scrooge” featuring Tommy Steele and the 2006 Lyric Theatre Recording of “Cabaret.”

In February 2008, he introduced the debut album of his new boy group, Dream On.

Early in his career, Kenwright ventured into acting and achieved success, including a role as Gordon Clegg in “Coronation Street,” introduced in April 1968.

Kenwright served as the chairman of Everton Football Club, taking over the role from Littlewoods director Philip Carter.

He joined the Everton board on October 23, 1989, and remained a part of it until his passing in 2023.

He became the second-largest shareholder in 1999, following Peter Johnson’s sale of his shareholding due to the Football Association’s directive to divest interests in either Tranmere Rovers or Everton.

In 2004, Kenwright became the largest stakeholder in the club.
